Wi-Fi Policy

1. Purpose

This policy is used to ensure every customer know the cost of using Wi-Fi in Mini-star.

2. Scope

All paying customers who are currently living in Mini-star.

3. Definitions

Wi- Fi

Wi-Fi, is a mechanism for wirelessly connecting electronic devices. A device enabled with Wi-Fi, such as a personal computer, video game console, smart phone, or digital audio player, can connect to the Internet via a wireless network access point. An access point (or hotspot) has a range of about 20 meters (65 ft) indoors and a greater range outdoors. Multiple overlapping access points can cover large areas.

MB: The megabyte is a multiple of the unit byte for digital information storage or transmission with two different values depending on context: 1048576 bytes (220) generally for computer memory; and one million bytes (106, see prefix mega-) generally for computer storage.

Who are customers?

A customer (also known as a client, buyer, or purchaser) is usually used to refer to a current or potential buyer or user of the products of an individual or organization, called the supplier, seller, or vendor. This is typically through purchasing or renting goods or services.

4. Policy Statement

All customer living in Mini-star has 20MB free Wi-Fi per day. If the customer run out of the 20 MB, he/she can purchase the charge extra quota at $1 for each megabyte.

5. Procedures

The customers will receive a user ID and password when they checks in. The customer will have to use this information to log in the ministar websit to use the Wi-Fi. After logging in system, the customer will have to locate an indicator on the right bottom corner on the system interface. It will show the customer MB balance and provide a warning at every 5MB usage. If the customer runs out of the free Wi-Fi, the system will automatically calculate the cost of extra wireless internet using. One extra megabyte will cost $1. The charging fee has to pay when customers check out.

Dress Policy

1. Purpose

The aim of this policy is to provide a guideline of proper dressing at the work place.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all staff members

  • Part-timestaff
  • Full-timestaff
  • Day-timestaff
  • Night-timestaff

3. Definitions

Staff Member

An employee who is a member of a staff of workers.

part Time Staff

An employee who works 20 hours a week.

Full Time Staff

An employee who works 40 hours a week.

Day Time Staff

An employee who works from 9am to 5pm.

Night Time Staff

An employee who works from 5pm to 1am.

Company Uniform

A prescribed identifying set of clothes for the members of an organisation.

Security Access Card

A plastic card with a magnetic strip containing encoded data that is read by passing the card through or over an electronic device, used especially to make electronic transactions and to provide access to restricted or secure areas.

Working Hours

The period of time that an individual spends at paid occupational labour.

4. Policy Statement

All staff members must wear company uniform and the security access card during working hours.

5. Procedures

Every staff member will receive two uniforms at the first day of work.

There onwards staff must wear their uniform at all time during the work. If in case they forget to bring their uniform, company will provide a free uniform for that day.If this happen three times, the staff will be charged $50.

Staff will receive a warning letter after three offences.

Any further offences after received a warning letter will face dismissal from company.
